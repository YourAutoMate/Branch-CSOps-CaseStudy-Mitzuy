Case Study: 90-Day Plan for Scaled Engagement and Revenue Protection
Prepared by Mitzuy Ley 1,000 Non-Managed Accounts 90 Days
Two things upfront. I'm making assumptions where I lack full visibility into Branch's current data quality, tooling maturity, and team workflows. I've noted those inline. This plan is also honest about what 90 days can realistically accomplish: a foundation that makes everything else possible, with a clear line between daily work and the revenue metrics that matter most.
Question 01
How I'd identify which accounts need attention and which are ready to grow

Before building any health scoring system, I'd audit what behavioral data actually exists in Looker and HubSpot and whether those systems talk to each other. I'm assuming they're not fully synced and that some fields exist in theory but aren't consistently populated. I start with signals that are reliable and add complexity as data quality improves.

I'd build a two-sided health score: one side flags accounts quietly disengaging, the other surfaces accounts showing growth signals nobody is acting on yet.

Risk signals
Transaction frequency: Two consecutive zero-payout weeks triggers an alert. Most businesses pay weekly or biweekly, so a missed cycle is meaningful, not noise.
Payout volume trend: A 20%+ sustained decline over 30 days. A one-week dip is not a signal. A pattern is.
Active user count: Fewer workers getting paid through Branch is a leading indicator. Workers disengage before businesses formally churn.
Failed transactions: Any failed payout unresolved after 48 hours erodes trust fast.
Compliance gaps: Outdated tax or bank details cause payment delays that look like disengagement but are actually solvable friction. Catching this early saves accounts that just need a quick admin fix.
Silent churn indicator: Zero account activity for 30+ days triggers high-priority intervention for Tiers 1, 2, and 3. Monthly review for Tier 4.
Growth signals
Revenue acceleration: Payout volume growing above portfolio average.
User growth: Expanding worker roster increases integration depth and switching cost.
Feature depth: 3+ features adopted means the account is embedded, not just testing.
Self-sufficiency: Low support tickets combined with strong growth often signals untapped expansion potential.
Health score formula
Health Score = (Revenue x 0.40) + (Engagement x 0.25) + (Risk x 0.20) + (Growth x 0.10) + (Compliance x 0.05)
Scale: 0 to 10. Tiers auto-update weekly. Manual review overrides any score when data gaps exist.
Tier routing
Tier 1: High Value, High Risk
40 to 50 accounts
Personal outreach from me. Maximum revenue at stake.
Tier 2: High Value, High Growth
150 to 200 accounts
Evaluated for Tier 1 promotion or Sales expansion signal report.
Tier 3: Lower Value, Declining
300 to 400 accounts
Automated, personalized re-engagement.
Tier 4: Dormant
400 to 500 accounts
Passive monitoring with quarterly reactivation and monthly risk review.

Prioritization rule: the health score tells me which 15 to 20 accounts need my attention this week. Everything else gets a program that is personal enough to feel relevant and scalable enough to run without me.

Question 02
What my scaled engagement model looks like

My philosophy: every outreach should deliver immediate, relevant value to the account receiving it. That means leading with their data, following with a recommendation grounded in their performance, and making it easy for them to take one clear next step. Generic outreach feels like marketing. A business manager dealing with 50 other things can tell the difference immediately.

Post-onboarding activation (Days 0 to 30)
Week 1: Setup confirmation and feature orientation.
Week 2: Every outreach is designed to deliver value. This touchpoint shares one actionable tip that makes the product more useful for how this specific business operates today.
Weeks 3 to 4: Activation check. Below baseline gets friction removal. Above baseline gets growth education.
Day 28 to 30: Tier assignment based on engagement velocity. This becomes the baseline for weekly health score updates.
Re-engagement (Tier 3)

The model: lead with a data point showing what is working for the account, then connect it to a recommendation backed by their own performance. For Branch, that sounds like: "Your team processed X payouts last month. We also noticed a few failed transactions in that window. Has anything shifted in your payout workflow? Here is how [Feature X] can help reduce that friction." One observation. One question grounded in their data. One recommendation.

Expansion (Tier 2)

Before routing any Tier 2 account to Sales, I first evaluate whether stronger engagement from me could unlock more growth. Accounts showing consistent acceleration with no recent personal touch get moved to Tier 1 for a direct conversation. Accounts that are clearly ready for the next level receive a structured expansion signal report to Sales: account, payout trend, user growth, feature adoption, and recommended timing. Tracked in HubSpot. Expansion signals auto-sync via webhook with no manual copy-paste. A weekly conversion dashboard tracks Flags to Opportunities to Closed deals.

Reactivation and churn management (Tier 4)

One re-engagement touch per quarter if reactivation signals appear, such as a leadership change, seasonal reopening, or new feature relevance. The goal is to maintain the relationship and remove barriers to return.

Inactivity decision timeline
Day 30
Tier 1 or 2: high-priority personal intervention. Tier 3: automated outreach. Tier 3 with no recovery potential: move to Tier 4 and include in monthly risk review.
Day 60
Graceful churn conversation. Acknowledge the relationship and confirm whether they want to continue. If signals recover, re-tier back to Tier 3.
Day 90
Schedule account for graceful churn. Document learnings: why did they go dormant? Was it a product gap, a market shift, or an internal change? Keep the door open.
Question 03
Where AI fits: two specific Claude workflows

The highest-value use of AI is replacing the work that happens before the actual work. The analysis, prioritization, and drafting that can consume 2 to 3 hours before a single customer interaction happens.

Workflow 1: Weekly Portfolio Intelligence Briefing (Monday 8am)
+
Scheduled Looker export (payout volume, transactions, active users) plus HubSpot snapshot sync feeds a structured Claude prompt
+
Claude flags accounts crossing risk thresholds, surfaces growth signals, outputs the top 10 accounts needing attention each with a one-sentence reason, flags unresolved failed transactions, and notes at-risk accounts with no recent contact
+
Output: structured briefing plus deep analysis covering health score refinement, signal testing, and learnings from the prior week
+
Safeguards: frequency caps, active escalation flags honored, Tier 4 marked for quarterly cadence only
1.5+ hours saved per week
Workflow 2: Personalized Outreach Engine (Tier 3 and growth campaigns)
+
Behavioral snapshot per account (payout trend, last active date, feature usage, last contact, open tickets) feeds a Claude prompt
+
Claude identifies the metric or data point driving value for this account (since every recommendation is backed by their own performance data), identifies the specific friction or missed opportunity, and drafts a short email opening with the value signal, following with the recommendation, and closing with one clear ask
+
Output is a personalized draft written around that account's behavioral data. I review, apply frequency caps, and send directly or approve for automated delivery for lower-tier accounts
4+ hours saved per week on drafting
Question 04
How I'd know if it's working
Day 30Visibility: can I see the portfolio clearly?
Percentage of 1,000 accounts with usable behavioral data
All accounts segmented into tiers
Silent churn signals mapped and baselined. A starting point, not a failure metric.

Adjustment trigger: if fewer than 60% of accounts have usable data, I simplify the health score to only what is reliable and work across teams to improve data capture.

Day 60Engagement: are programs moving behavior?
Campaign open rates above 20%
Tier 1 personal outreach response rate
Feature adoption lift in activation sequences
Weekly briefing accuracy vs. manual priority

Adjustment trigger: if re-engagement campaigns are not converting, I'd revisit the subject line, email copy, and send timing through A/B testing to identify what needs to shift.

Day 90Revenue impact: is the model protecting growth?
Percentage of flagged accounts that stabilized or improved
Recovery rate for Tier 3 re-engagement
Hours saved per week from automation versus manual baseline
Expansion signals surfaced and converted by Sales

Adjustment trigger: if behavioral retention is not improving after 60 days, I'd collaborate with Product and Support to investigate systemic issues.

Question 05
How I'd personally execute in the early days

Before any automation is built and before any playbook is written, the most important thing I can do is listen.

My first priority is completing onboarding and using that time to build real relationships with the people I will be working alongside. I'd request 1:1s with Megan, Brian, Sales, CX Ops, the GTM CRM Administrator, Support, and Product. My goal would be to hear what is working well and should not be changed, where the manual friction is worst, and what the team already knows from experience that could be standardized into something repeatable for everyone.

I'd also shadow live interactions with business accounts and end user support conversations to understand the customer experience firsthand.

Weeks 1 to 2
Listen. Nothing built yet. At the end of week 2, I'd share a brief written summary with leadership covering what I heard, what it means, and what I am building first and why.
Weeks 3 to 5
Build health scoring v1 in Looker and HubSpot using the most reliable signals available. Run the Monday briefing for myself before sharing it. Build Tier 3 re-engagement templates and data mapping. Test the Claude personalization workflow on 50 accounts. Capacity model: 65% planned engagement, 35% reactive. Validate and adjust this split based on actual volume.
Weeks 6 to 9
Tier 3 re-engagement sequences live, batched weekly. Tier 4 reactivation cadence live. Personal Tier 1 outreach underway. Weekly expansion signal report to Sales. Monitoring open rates, response rates, feature adoption, and tier progression.
Weeks 10 to 12
Reflect, iterate, and document. Every system gets written up (Claude prompts, health score logic, playbook reasoning, workflows) so nothing is dependent on me being in the room. Executive retrospective sharing impact, ROI, results from initial tests, and learnings from piloting the scaled engagement model.

The accounts that churn quietly almost always gave a signal that nobody was positioned to see. The accounts with the most growth potential are often the ones not making any noise, easy to overlook when the whole team is in reactive mode.

By Day 90, this system is operational: playbooks are documented, tiers auto-update weekly, the briefing is running, and there is measurable evidence of revenue protection, expansion pipeline creation, and thoughtful churn management. The foundation built here becomes the operating system for everything the CS team builds next.

I care deeply about getting this right. To me that means building something intentional from day one. I am driven to create a system the team actually wants to use, that keeps getting better over time, and that makes it a little easier to show up for every account in the portfolio.
