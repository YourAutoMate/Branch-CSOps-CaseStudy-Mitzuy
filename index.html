90-Day Plan: Scaled Engagement
and Revenue Protection

A data-driven motion that surfaces risk early, routes accounts to the right lifecycle program, and scales personalized engagement through AI automation.

Mitzuy Ley 1,000 Non-Managed Accounts 90 Days
Before I Begin

Two things upfront. I'm making assumptions where I lack full visibility into Branch's current data quality, tooling maturity, and team workflows. I've noted those inline. This plan is honest about what 90 days can realistically accomplish: a foundation that makes everything else possible, with a clear line between daily work and the revenue metrics that matter most.

Question 01

How I'd identify which accounts need attention and which are ready to grow

Before building any health scoring system, I'd audit what behavioral data actually exists in Looker and HubSpot and whether those systems talk to each other. I'm assuming they're not fully synced and that some fields exist in theory but aren't consistently populated. I start with signals that are reliable and add complexity as data quality improves.

Risk Signals
Transaction frequency: Two consecutive zero-payout weeks triggers an alert.
Payout volume trend: 20%+ sustained decline over 30 days.
Active user count: Workers disengage before businesses formally churn.
Failed transactions: Unresolved after 48 hours erodes trust fast.
Compliance gaps: Outdated tax or bank details causing payment delays.
Silent churn: Zero activity for 30+ days triggers intervention.
Growth Signals
Revenue acceleration: Payout volume growing above portfolio average.
User growth: Expanding worker roster increases integration depth.
Feature depth: 3+ features adopted signals deep embedding.
Self-sufficiency: Low support tickets combined with strong growth signals untapped potential.
Health Score Formula
Health Score = (Revenue × 0.40) + (Engagement × 0.25) + (Risk × 0.20) + (Growth × 0.10) + (Compliance × 0.05)
Scale: 0 to 10. Tiers auto-update weekly. Manual review overrides when data gaps exist.
Tier Routing
Tier 1: High Value, High Risk
40–50 accounts
Personal outreach from me. Maximum revenue at stake.
Tier 2: High Value, High Growth
150–200 accounts
Evaluated for Tier 1 promotion or Sales expansion signal report.
Tier 3: Lower Value, Declining
300–400 accounts
Automated, personalized re-engagement.
Tier 4: Dormant
400–500 accounts
Passive monitoring, quarterly reactivation, monthly risk review.

Prioritization rule: the health score identifies the 15 to 20 accounts needing my attention this week. Everything else gets a program personal enough to feel relevant and scalable enough to run without me.

Question 02

What my scaled engagement model looks like

Every outreach should deliver immediate, relevant value to the account receiving it. Lead with their data. Follow with a recommendation grounded in their performance. Make it easy to take one clear next step.
Post-Onboarding Activation (Days 0 to 30)
Week 1
Setup confirmation and feature orientation.
Week 2
Every outreach is designed to deliver value. One actionable tip that makes the product more useful for how this specific business operates today.
Weeks 3–4
Activation check. Below baseline gets friction removal. Above baseline gets growth education.
Day 28–30
Tier assignment based on engagement velocity. Becomes the baseline for weekly health score updates.
Re-Engagement (Tier 3)

Lead with a data point showing what is working for the account, then connect it to a recommendation backed by their own performance. "Your team processed X payouts last month. We also noticed a few failed transactions in that window. Has anything shifted in your payout workflow? Here is how [Feature X] can help reduce that friction." One observation. One question. One recommendation.

Expansion (Tier 2)

Before routing any Tier 2 account to Sales, I evaluate whether stronger engagement from me could unlock more growth. Accounts showing consistent acceleration with no recent personal touch get moved to Tier 1. Accounts ready for the next level receive a structured expansion signal report: account, payout trend, user growth, feature adoption, recommended timing. Expansion signals auto-sync via webhook. A weekly conversion dashboard tracks Flags to Opportunities to Closed deals.

Inactivity Decision Timeline
Day 30
Tier 1 or 2: high-priority personal intervention. Tier 3: automated outreach. Tier 3 with no recovery potential: move to Tier 4 and include in monthly risk review.
Day 60
Graceful churn conversation. Acknowledge the relationship and confirm whether they want to continue. If signals recover, re-tier back to Tier 3.
Day 90
Schedule account for graceful churn. Document learnings: why did they go dormant? Was it a product gap, a market shift, or an internal change? Keep the door open.

Question 04

How I'd know if it's working

I measure in phases because the right questions change as the program matures.

Day 30
Visibility: can I see the portfolio clearly?
  • Percentage of accounts with usable behavioral data
  • All accounts segmented into tiers
  • Silent churn signals mapped and baselined
  • Health score thresholds tested
Adjustment: simplify health score to reliable signals if data coverage is below 60%.
Day 60
Engagement: are programs moving behavior?
  • Campaign open rates above 20%
  • Tier 1 personal outreach response rate
  • Feature adoption lift in activation sequences
  • Weekly briefing accuracy vs. manual priority
Adjustment: A/B test subject lines, email copy, and send timing to identify what needs to shift.
Day 90
Revenue impact: is the model protecting growth?
  • Percentage of flagged accounts stabilized or improved
  • Recovery rate for Tier 3 re-engagement
  • Hours saved vs. manual baseline
  • Expansion signals converted by Sales
Adjustment: collaborate with Product and Support to investigate systemic issues.
Question 05

How I'd personally execute in the early days

Before any automation is built and before any playbook is written, the most important thing I can do is listen. My first priority is completing onboarding and using that time to build real relationships with the people I will be working alongside. My goal would be to hear what is working well and should not be changed, where the manual friction is worst, and what the team already knows from experience that could be standardized into something repeatable for everyone. I would also shadow live interactions with business accounts and end user support conversations to understand the customer experience firsthand.

Weeks 1–2
Listen. 1:1s with Megan, Brian, Sales, CX Ops, GTM CRM Administrator, Support, and Product. Nothing built yet. Written summary to leadership at the end of week 2.
Weeks 3–5
Build health scoring v1 in Looker and HubSpot. Run Monday briefing privately before sharing. Build Tier 3 re-engagement templates. Test Claude personalization on 50 accounts. Capacity model: 65% planned, 35% reactive.
Weeks 6–9
Tier 3 re-engagement sequences live, batched weekly. Tier 4 reactivation cadence live. Personal Tier 1 outreach underway. Weekly expansion signal report to Sales.
Weeks 10–12
Reflect, iterate, and document. Every system gets written up so nothing is dependent on me being in the room. Executive retrospective sharing impact, ROI, test results, and scaled engagement learnings.
Why this approach lasts

The accounts that churn quietly almost always gave a signal that nobody was positioned to see.

The accounts with the most growth potential are often the ones not making any noise, easy to overlook when the whole team is in reactive mode. By Day 90, this system is operational: playbooks are documented, tiers auto-update weekly, the briefing is running, and there is measurable evidence of revenue protection, expansion pipeline creation, and thoughtful churn management. The foundation built here becomes the operating system for everything the CS team builds next.

Getting this right matters to me. It means building the infrastructure for scaled account management intentionally from day one. I am driven by the idea that when the foundation is built right, it'll keep delivering value long after the work to build it is done.
